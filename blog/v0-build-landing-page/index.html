How to Build a Landing Page With v0 by Vercel: Complete Tutorial (2026)

A well-designed landing page can be the difference between a product that converts and one that does not. With v0 by Vercel, you can generate a complete, professional landing page — hero, features, social proof, pricing, and footer — in under 30 minutes without writing a line of CSS.

This tutorial walks through every section with the exact prompts we use, the iteration techniques that work best, and the final deployment steps. By the end you will have a real, live landing page on a Vercel URL.

What You Will Build

A complete SaaS landing page with:

  • Sticky navigation bar with logo and CTA
  • Hero section with headline, subheadline, two CTAs, and trust badges
  • Three-column features grid with icons
  • Social proof section (logos or testimonials)
  • Pricing cards (Free, Pro, Enterprise)
  • Final CTA banner
  • Footer with links and copyright

All built with React, Tailwind CSS, and shadcn/ui. Fully responsive on mobile. Deployable to Vercel with one click.

Before You Start

You need a v0 account (free tier works for this tutorial — the whole page will cost roughly $1–2 in credits) and a rough idea of what your product does. You do not need design skills, Figma files, or any existing code.

Decide on these before starting — it will help you write better prompts:

  • Your product name and one-sentence description
  • Your color scheme (or just say "modern dark" or "clean white")
  • Three main features you want to highlight
  • Your pricing structure (even roughly)

Step 1: Generate the Hero Section

Start a new v0 chat and use this prompt structure, customized for your product:

"Create a SaaS landing page hero section for [PRODUCT NAME] — [one sentence description]. Include: a bold headline (8–10 words max), a supporting subheadline (15–20 words), a primary CTA button labeled '[YOUR CTA]', a secondary ghost button labeled 'See how it works', and a row of three trust badges showing [relevant social proof]. Dark background, [YOUR COLOR] accent, clean modern design with generous whitespace."

The key to a great hero prompt: be specific about every element. If you leave out the trust badges, you will not get them. If you do not specify button labels, you get placeholder text you have to change later.

After generation, check: Does the headline communicate value immediately? Is the CTA prominent? Does the layout feel balanced? If anything is off, add a follow-up prompt: "Make the headline larger. Move the trust badges below the CTAs. Add more vertical padding."

Step 2: Add a Features Grid

In the same chat (very important — v0 remembers the context), add:

"Below the hero, add a features section with a centered headline 'Everything you need to [OUTCOME]' and a three-column grid. Each feature card should have a small icon, a bold feature name, and two sentences of description. Features: 1) [FEATURE 1 NAME] — [description]. 2) [FEATURE 2 NAME] — [description]. 3) [FEATURE 3 NAME] — [description]."

Specifying actual feature names and descriptions in the prompt produces real content instead of placeholder text, which saves you Design Mode time later. Use real icons that match your product domain — v0 has access to Lucide icons, so specify which ones you want: "use the Zap, Shield, and BarChart icons."

Step 3: Add Social Proof

Social proof is one of the highest-converting elements on a landing page. Options that work well in v0:

Logo strip — A row of company logos that use your product:

"Add a social proof section with the text 'Trusted by teams at' followed by a horizontal row of six company name logos in a muted gray style: Notion, Linear, Stripe, Vercel, Figma, GitHub."

Testimonials — A grid of three testimonial cards:

"Add a testimonials section with three cards in a row. Each card has a quote, the person's name, title, and company. Use realistic placeholder testimonials relevant to [YOUR PRODUCT CATEGORY]."

Step 4: Add Pricing Cards

Pricing sections are among the most credit-efficient generations in v0 because the structure is well-defined. Use this prompt:

"Add a pricing section with a centered heading 'Simple, transparent pricing' and three pricing cards side by side. Free: $0/month, 5 features listed, CTA 'Get started free'. Pro: $29/month (highlighted as most popular with a badge), 8 features, CTA 'Start free trial'. Enterprise: Custom pricing, unlimited features, CTA 'Contact sales'. Use checkmarks for features. Highlight the Pro card with a border in the accent color."

After generating, update the actual features list in Design Mode — click each feature item and replace the placeholder text with your real feature names.

Step 5: Add a CTA Banner and Footer

Close the page with a high-contrast CTA section and a professional footer:

"Add a full-width CTA banner with a bold headline 'Start building today', a subheadline 'No credit card required. Cancel anytime.', and a prominent CTA button. Use a subtle gradient or accent background to make it stand out. Below that, add a footer with four columns: Product (links: Features, Pricing, Changelog), Company (About, Blog, Careers), Legal (Privacy, Terms), and Social icons (Twitter/X, GitHub, LinkedIn)."

Step 6: Add a Navigation Bar

Add the nav last so it reflects the final structure of the page:

"Add a sticky navigation bar at the top with a logo on the left ([PRODUCT NAME] in bold with a small icon), three nav links in the center (Features, Pricing, Blog), and a CTA button on the right (Sign up free). Make it semi-transparent with a blur backdrop on scroll."

Step 7: Refine in Design Mode

Switch to Design Mode (paintbrush icon) and go through the page to:

  • Replace all placeholder text with your real copy
  • Adjust the primary accent color to match your brand
  • Fix any spacing issues by adjusting padding values
  • Resize the preview to check mobile layout

Design Mode costs zero credits. Spend as long as you need here — it is where a generic template becomes your landing page.

Step 8: Deploy

When you are happy with the result, click Deploy in the v0 interface. Choose or create a Vercel project, confirm, and click Deploy. You will have a live HTTPS URL in under 90 seconds. Share it, get feedback, and iterate. The full deployment guide covers custom domains, environment variables, and more.
