v0 Free Tier Limits Explained: What You Actually Get for Free in 2026

v0 by Vercel has a genuinely useful free tier — not a crippled demo. You can build real components, iterate on them, and deploy them to Vercel without spending a dollar. But the free tier does have limits, and understanding them helps you avoid running out of credits at an inconvenient moment.

This guide covers every aspect of v0's free tier: what costs credits, what is always free, what the hard limits are, and how to maximize what you get for $0/month.

Free Tier Overview

v0's free tier (as of April 2026) includes:

  • $5.00 in monthly credits — resets on your billing anniversary each month
  • Unlimited Design Mode edits — never costs credits
  • Unlimited project creation — no cap on how many projects you have
  • GitHub sync — available on free tier
  • Vercel deployment — available on free tier (subject to Vercel's own free plan limits)
  • Access to all models — free tier uses v0's standard model

There is no credit card required to sign up and no automatic charge when you hit your limit. You simply cannot generate new AI content until the next month's credits reload (or you upgrade).

How Credits Work

v0 uses a credit system measured in dollars. Each AI generation consumes a certain amount of credits depending on the complexity of the request. Credits are not a fixed number of "generations" — a simple one-component prompt costs less than generating a full multi-page application.

Credit consumption is roughly:

  • Simple component (a button group, a card): ~$0.03–$0.08
  • Medium component (a full hero section, a form): ~$0.08–$0.20
  • Full page (landing page with multiple sections): ~$0.20–$0.50
  • Full application (multi-page app via Agentic mode): ~$0.50–$2.00+

This means $5 in free credits translates to roughly: 60–150 simple component generations, or 25–60 medium generations, or 10–25 full page generations, or 2–10 full application builds. Most users find the free tier sufficient for exploration and light prototyping.

What Uses Credits

Credits are consumed by any request that calls the AI generation engine:

  • Initial component/page generation from a prompt
  • Follow-up AI chat prompts (refine, add, change)
  • Image-to-code conversion (uploading a screenshot and asking v0 to recreate it)
  • Agentic mode tasks (multi-step autonomous generation)
  • Code explanation requests ("explain this component")

What Is Always Free (Zero Credits)

These features never consume credits regardless of your plan:

  • Design Mode — all visual editing: text changes, color adjustments, spacing, layout
  • Previewing generated components
  • Resizing the preview to check responsiveness
  • Toggling dark/light mode in the preview
  • Viewing and copying code from generated components
  • GitHub sync setup and pushes
  • Vercel deployment (uses Vercel credits/plan, not v0 credits)
  • Project management — creating, renaming, organizing projects

Design Mode is particularly valuable: most content-level refinements (replacing placeholder text, changing colors, adjusting copy) can be done entirely in Design Mode at zero cost. See our Design Mode guide to get the most out of this.

Hard Limits on the Free Tier

Beyond credit consumption, the free tier has a few feature restrictions:

  • Priority access — free tier users may experience slower generation times during peak hours; paid users get priority queue
  • Team collaboration — real-time multiplayer editing and team workspaces require a paid plan
  • Advanced models — some model options may be limited to paid plans
  • Private projects — free tier projects are private by default, but there may be limits on project count at some thresholds

What Happens When You Hit the Limit

When your $5 monthly credit balance reaches zero, v0 stops processing AI generation requests. Specifically:

  • New chat prompts will show a "Credit limit reached" message
  • Design Mode continues to work normally
  • You can still view, copy, and export previously generated code
  • GitHub sync and Vercel deployment continue to work
  • Credits reset on your monthly billing date (check your account settings for the exact date)

There is no overage — you will not be charged automatically. You can either wait for the monthly reset or upgrade to a paid plan to continue generating immediately.

How to Stretch Your Free Credits

With some discipline, $5/month is enough for active use. Here are the techniques that make the biggest difference:

Use Design Mode for all content changes. Once a component's structure is right, switch to Design Mode for all text, color, and spacing adjustments. This single habit can cut your credit consumption by 40–60%.

Write detailed prompts the first time. Vague initial prompts require more follow-up iterations. A thorough first prompt that specifies layout, colors, elements, and style often gets you 80% of the way there in one generation. See our prompting guide for the exact framework we use.

Build components, not pages. Generating a full page with many sections costs more than generating sections individually and composing them. Break your UI into components and generate each one separately.

Supplement with Claude Artifacts. When your v0 credits run low, Claude Artifacts (free on claude.ai) handles simple component requests well. Use it for quick, one-off components and save your v0 credits for complex generations.

Save and reuse your best generations. v0 stores your project history, but if you find a component structure you love, save the code locally. You will not need to regenerate it — you can use it as a starting point by pasting it back with "update this component to..."

When the Free Tier Is Not Enough

The $5 free tier becomes a constraint when you are:

  • Generating 10+ components per week as part of an active project
  • Using Agentic mode for multi-page application generation
  • On a deadline and cannot wait for the monthly credit reset
  • Working in a team and needing real-time collaboration
  • Processing image-to-code conversions regularly

In these cases, the $20/month Premium plan is usually worth it. Read our full v0 pricing guide and our honest review of whether v0 is worth paying for before deciding.
