Is Vercel V0 Really Free? Credits, Limits & What You Can Build

V0 has a free tier. But $5/month in credits runs out faster than you'd expect. Here's exactly what you can build for free, and the honest case for upgrading.

Short answer: Yes, V0 is genuinely free to start — no credit card required. You get $5 in monthly credits that reset each billing cycle. That's enough to explore the tool and build simple components, but heavy users and complex projects will hit the ceiling fast.

All plans include access to all three AI model tiers (Mini, Pro, and Max). The difference is how many credits you get each month and whether you can purchase more.

How credits actually work (the part people miss)

Before May 2025, V0 charged by message count — you had a fixed number of generations per month. Vercel replaced this with token-based metered billing, which caused significant backlash from the developer community.

Now, every generation consumes tokens based on prompt size, output size, and which AI model you chose. Those tokens are converted into credits from your monthly allowance.

Always check which model is selected before generating.

What the free tier includes (beyond credits)

  • Access to all three AI models (Mini, Pro, Max)
  • Deploy to Vercel with a preview URL
  • GitHub sync
  • Design Mode (visual editing)
  • Code export — always free, no paywall
  • Version history

The free tier is notably generous compared to many competitors. You can export your code without any paywall — unlike Lovable, which requires a paid plan for code download.

When to upgrade to Premium ($20/month)

Premium makes sense when:

  • You are building more than 3–5 landing pages or complex components per month
  • You want to use Figma import (Premium feature)
  • You need V0 API access to integrate generation into your own workflow
  • You keep hitting the free credit wall mid-project
  • You want the ability to purchase additional credits when needed
Premium math: At $20/month you get $20 in credits — the same dollar amount as the plan cost. That feels like you're paying for the platform fee and getting the credits free. In practice, $20 in credits covers several medium-complexity projects or many simple component generations per month.

Can I buy more credits without upgrading?

No. On the free plan, once your $5 runs out you must wait for the monthly reset or upgrade. Only Premium, Team, and Enterprise users can purchase additional credits on demand. Purchased credits do not expire at the end of the billing month — they roll over until used.

Tips to make free credits last longer

  1. Use Mini for iterations. Once you have a base component, use Mini to refine rather than regenerating from scratch with Max.
  2. Be specific on the first prompt. Vague prompts lead to outputs you'll regenerate. One good detailed prompt beats three vague ones.
  3. Use V0 for UI, write logic yourself. Don't ask V0 to generate complex business logic — it's best at visual components. Keep prompts focused on layout and UI.
  4. Monitor the model selector. V0 sometimes auto-selects a higher tier for complex requests. Check before submitting.

Frequently asked questions

Is Vercel V0 free?

Yes. V0 has a free tier with $5 in monthly credits that resets every billing cycle. No credit card is required to start. The free credits are enough to explore the tool and build simple components, but complex projects will deplete them quickly.

How much does V0 cost per month?

Free ($0 with $5 credits), Premium ($20/month with $20 credits), Team ($30/user/month with $30 credits per user), and Enterprise (custom pricing). Additional credits can be purchased on paid plans.

How fast do credits run out?

Using V0 Mini for simple components, $5 can last weeks. Using V0 Pro or Max for complex multi-page layouts, some users deplete $5 in a single session. The May 2025 switch to token-based billing made costs less predictable than the old message-count model.

Can I use V0 without a credit card?

Yes. The free tier requires only a Vercel account — no payment information needed. You only need a credit card if you upgrade to a paid plan or want to purchase additional credits.
